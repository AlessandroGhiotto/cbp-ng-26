It evaluates both scalar and block-based versions for all families (including the newly added `papL` and `lxorL`).

## 4. Key Performance Gains from Parameter Optimization

The optimized configurations resulted in substantial VFS performance improvements:

### A. GAP Predictor Breakthrough (Scalar, 8KB)

By shifting the bit allocation from `gap<8,7,2>` (which carries a 2-cycle latency penalty) to `gap<5,10,2>`, the critical path latency dropped below 300ps.

- **VFS Score**: Doubled from **`0.1212`** to **`0.2338`** on average.
- **Rank**: The optimized GAP is now the **best scalar predictor** at 8KB, outperforming the previously best TAGE (`0.2254`).
- **Insight**: For simple predictors, reducing history length to shrink register shift overhead and path delay provides a massive IPC boost that dwarfs the minor accuracy loss.

### B. Perceptron Optimization

Optimizing the BHR length vs PC index entries led to large gains:

- **Perceptron Scalar (8KB)**: VFS score improved from **`0.0841`** to **`0.1197`** by using shorter history length (`BHR=15`) which reduces training and computing latency.
- **Perceptron Block LI=4 (8KB)**: VFS score improved from **`0.1859`** to **`0.3502`** (a **1.88x speedup**).
- **Perceptron Block LI=16 (8KB)**: VFS score improved from **`0.3145`** to **`0.3756`**.

### C. PAgL Block Sizing (16KB)

For PAgL, shifting from `<11, 12, 2, 4>` to `<12, 2, 2, 4>` (larger local history table, smaller BHR table) improved VFS from **`0.3165`** to **`0.5672`** (a **1.8x speedup**).

### D. PApL & LXORL Block Performance (8KB/16KB)

The newly added block predictors achieve excellent VFS scores through parameter optimization:

- **PApL (Block)**: Shifting to `papL<8,10,3,2,4>` (16KB) achieves a mean VFS of **`0.6096`**, representing a **5.1x speedup** over scalar PAP.
- **LXORL (Block)**: Shifting to `lxorL<12,5,4>` (8KB/16KB) achieves a mean VFS of **`0.5948`**, representing a **4.98x speedup** over scalar LXOR.
